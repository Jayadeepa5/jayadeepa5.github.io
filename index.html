About Me

I am an Automotive Cybersecurity Consultant at Breachlabz, working on cybersecurity frameworks for automotive systems with hands-on involvement in SUMS work product development and practical knowledge of ISO/SAE 24089 and UN R156 compliance requirements.

My foundation in Automotive Functional Safety — spanning ISO 26262 work products across 2W EV platforms, occupant restraint, exterior lighting, and wash-wipe for 4W PHEV — gives me a rigorous, systems-level perspective that most pure-security practitioners lack.

I operate at the intersection of cybersecurity, functional safety, and compliance, understanding how security vulnerabilities interact with safety-critical vehicle architectures. My dual background lets me reason across both domains with technical precision.

Standards & Frameworks

ISO/SAE 21434Cybersecurity
Road Vehicles — Cybersecurity Engineering
Foundational automotive cybersecurity standard. Covers CSMS requirements, TARA methodology, and the full cybersecurity engineering lifecycle for road vehicles and their supply chains.
ISO/SAE 24089SW Update Eng.
Road Vehicles — Software Update Engineering
Engineering standard for software update management across the vehicle lifecycle. Covers SUMS design, verification, and OTA update processes aligned to UN R156 regulatory requirements.
UN R155CSMS Regulation
Cyber Security Management System (CSMS)
UNECE regulation mandating a Cyber Security Management System for vehicle type approval. Defines obligations for OEMs and the supply chain throughout the vehicle lifecycle.
UN R156SUMS Regulation
Software Update Management System (SUMS)
UNECE regulation requiring a Software Update Management System to ensure software updates do not compromise vehicle safety, security, or environmental performance. Hands-on work product experience.
ISO 26262Pts 3 · 7 · 9 · 11
Road Vehicles — Functional Safety
International standard for functional safety of E/E systems. Hands-on experience: concept phase (Pt 3), production (Pt 7), ASIL-oriented analysis (Pt 9), and semiconductor safety (Pt 11).
